When I jumped into the big, crazy, amazing world of freelancing and building websites for clients I was so excited to get to do what I loved from the comfort of my home. But there was a flaw in my business and I didn’t know it â€” that was until one month I only made $708.75. That’s not even enough to cover rent.

The problem? I was only building static websites, and performing front-end development work.

There were two types of jobs I would get:

1) I’d code static websites, or HTML & CSS templates and invoice for up to $2k per project.

2) I’d get a dynamic website request and I would code the HTML, CSS and JS then pay a back-end developer to finish the site up for me and hook it into a CMS, like WordPress. In the end, I’d pay the developer around $1k – $3k and net only $1k – $2k for myself.

There were times I even rejected an opportunity because it involved WordPress development!

What’s wrong with that scenario? Well, for one, talk about MISSED OPPORTUNITIES! Two, I’d pay thousands of dollars out-of-pocket to have somebody finish my work for me. And most crucially, I let my FEAR of learning WordPress get in the way of me growing my business, getting hired by premium clientele, and making 2x – 3x more money every single month.

…silly Kiran!

In my first year of business, I’d calculated that I’d spent around $8,000 to pay for WordPress developers, and I’d missed around $16,000 in potential business as a WordPress developer.

Let me break this down for you:

In a 12-month period, there were around 5 WordPress projects I paid a developer $1k – $3k to build. Grand total: $8,000!

In that same period, there were at least 8 requests to build a WordPress website I could have easily charged $2,000+ for. Total in missed business? $16,000.

Shortly into my second year of business I decided enough was enough, and I had to stop letting my fear of WordPress from getting in the way of me growing my business, getting hired by premium clientele, and making more money.

Since then, building custom WordPress websites, and updating custom WordPress websites account for 99% of my freelance business. And all that money I shelled out to pay WordPress developers? It now goes straight into my pocket, so I can rest assured I can provide for family, as we are only a single income household. No more $708.75 months!
